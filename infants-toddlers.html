Infants & Toddlers (Ages 0–2)

Building Life’s Foundation

Developmental Overview

The first two years of life represent the most rapid period of brain development in the human lifespan. Neural connections form at an astonishing rate, laying the foundation for future learning, health, and emotional well-being (Shonkoff & Phillips, 2000). Secure attachment, motor milestones, and early language exposure are especially critical during this time.

Physical Development

Key Milestones

  • Rolling over, sitting, crawling, walking (typically by 12–15 months)
  • Fine motor skills such as grasping, pincer grip, self-feeding
  • Rapid growth in height and weight, with steady head growth reflecting brain development

Activities for Families

  • Tummy time: Strengthens core and prepares for crawling
  • Outdoor walks in Jefferson County parks: Provides sensory experiences and motor practice
  • Safe climbing and crawling spaces: Build coordination and balance

Cognitive Development

According to Piaget, infants move through the sensorimotor stage, learning through direct interaction with the environment (Piaget, 1952). Object permanence, imitation, and cause-and-effect understanding develop during this period.

  • Peek-a-boo games to support object permanence
  • Cause-and-effect toys (blocks, rattles, water play)
  • Reading simple picture books daily to promote language pathways

Social-Emotional Development

Attachment relationships form the foundation of social and emotional health. Consistent, responsive caregiving helps children develop trust and emotional regulation skills (Bowlby, 1969).

  • Respond promptly to cries and cues to build secure attachment
  • Face-to-face play and mirroring games to foster bonding
  • Introduce other caregivers gradually to support adaptation
